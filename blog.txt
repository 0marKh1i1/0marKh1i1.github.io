## Overview

On December 5th and 6th, I had the pleasure of conducting a focused, two-day online workshop in collaboration with the **IEEE Robotics & Automation Society (JUST Student Chapter)**. The goal was simple but ambitious: bridge the gap between academic theory and the real-world technical skills required in the tech industry.

As a Computer Engineer and DevOps practitioner, I know that **Linux** is not just an operating system; it is the backbone of the internet, cloud computing, and robotics. This workshop was designed to take students from "fearing the black screen" to confidently automating tasks and managing systems.

<br /><br />
## Day 1: The Foundation & Philosophy

<SmartImage
  src="https://youtu.be/MmS7yb6zKQs?si=tk7Fot8lKLjQQmaE"
  alt="Linux Workshop Day 1 Recording"
  aspectRatio="16/9"
  radius="l"
/>
<br />
The first session was dedicated to breaking the ice with the terminal and understanding *why* we use Linux. We explored the history of **Linus Torvalds** and the **GNU Project**, explaining Free Open Source Software (FOSS).

**Key Topics Covered:**
* **Linux vs. Windows**: Understanding the file system structure (Root vs. C: Drive) and privacy implications.
* **The Terminal**: Navigating the system with `pwd`, `ls`, and `cd` instead of a mouse.
* **File Operations**: Creating, moving, and manipulating files using `touch`, `cp`, `mv`, and `rm`.
* **Package Management**: The power of `apt` vs. downloading .exe files.

We ended the session with a competitive **Kahoot!** quiz to reinforce the concepts.

## Day 2: Advanced Skills & Scripting

<SmartImage
  src="https://youtu.be/ug7J7TZHU10?si=30MX0_ZkGIRFHwQl"
  alt="Linux Workshop Day 2 Recording"
  aspectRatio="16/9"

<br />
The second day focused on power-user features and development workflows. We moved past basic navigation into actual automation and remote management—skills essential for any DevOps or Robotics role.

**Key Topics Covered:**
* **Advanced Streams**: Using `grep` for searching and the pipe operator `|` to chain commands together.
* **Permissions**: Understanding `chmod` (symbolic and octal modes), `chown`, and the responsibility of `sudo`.
* **Bash Scripting**: We built a functional **Calculator Script** from scratch using variables, logic (`if/else`), and arguments.
* **SSH & Networking**: An introduction to connecting to remote servers, demonstrated using the **OverTheWire Bandit** wargame.

## Outcome

The workshop saw great engagement, with students successfully writing their first Bash scripts and navigating the file system without a GUI. By demystifying the terminal, we've empowered a new group of students to explore **Robotics (ROS)**, **Cloud Computing**, and **Software Development** with the industry-standard tools they need to succeed.
